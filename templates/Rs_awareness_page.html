Awareness Days

Few common awareness days

1. Breast Cancer Awareness

Breast cancer is one of the most common cancers in women. Early detection through regular self-exams and screenings can save lives.

2. PCOS Awareness

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) affects many young women, leading to irregular periods, weight issues, and hormonal imbalances. Lifestyle changes and treatment can help manage it effectively.

3. World Mental Health Day

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Talking about stress, anxiety, and depression helps break stigma and encourages people to seek support.

4. World Diabetes Day

Diabetes is a growing health concern worldwide. Healthy eating, regular exercise, and routine check-ups play a big role in prevention and management.

5. Hypertension Awareness

High blood pressure often has no symptoms but increases the risk of heart disease and stroke. Reducing salt, staying active, and regular monitoring are essential.

6. Cervical Cancer Awareness

Cervical cancer can often be prevented through Pap smear tests and HPV vaccination. Early detection makes treatment more successful.

7. World Heart Day

Heart disease is one of the leading causes of death. Eating healthy, staying active, and avoiding smoking are key to a healthy heart.
